Thermoplastics and Thermosets


There are many different types of plastics, and they can be grouped into two main polymer families:

Thermoplastics:

Thermoplastics are polymers that can be softened and melted by heat. While in the softened state it can be processed by thermoforming, and in the liquid state by extrusion and injection molding

Processing: The processing of thermoplastics is reversible as no chemical bonding takes place, and they can be remolded with no negative effects.

Thermosets:

Thermoset plastics are synthetic materials that are strengthened when heated but lack the ability to be remolded or reheated.

Processing: These plastics contain polymers that cross-link during the curing process and form an irreversible chemical bond. Since they cannot be reheated, thermosets are the perfect candidates for high-heat applications like electronics and appliances.

Amorphous plastics

Amorphous plastics are clear, easily thermoform, bond strongly with the use of adhesives, and soften over a range of temperatures. However, they are prone to stress cracking and don’t resist fatigue. They are more stable in comparison to Semi Crystalline plastics. These plastics are acrylonitrile butadiene styrene, polycarbonate, polyethylene, terephthalate glycol, acrylic, polysulfone.


Semi Crystalline plastics

Semi Crystalline plastics are plastics that don’t degrade easily and make useful as structural applications. However, they don’t thermoform easily, resist bonding, have typical resistance to impact, and have a sharp melting point. They have better resistance to chemicals, stronger electrical properties, and a lower coefficient of friction in comparison to Amorphous plastics. These plastics are polyethylene, polypropylene, nylon, acetal, and fluoropolymers.
