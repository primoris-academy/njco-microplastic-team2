Main Plastics

There are seven main types of plastics, these are the ones that we use in our everyday life and are the most produced plastic variants.

Source: https://dienamics.com.au/lets-talk-about-polyethylene-terephthalate/

Polyethylene terephthalate (PETE or PET) is the most widely produced plastic in the world. For example, PET is used for bottled water and polyester fabric.
Polyethylene (PE) has number of variants: LDPE used for mostly plastic bags in grocery stores, HDPE used for packaging like laundry detergent containers, and UHMW used for extremely strong plastic that can rival steel in strength like artificial hips.

Source: https://www.bobvila.com/articles/how-to-paint-pvc-pipe/

Polyvinyl Chloride (PVC) is the world’s third most mass-produced plastic. It is used to make pipes (PVC pipes) and serves a variety of functions in construction and automobile assembly.
Polypropylene (PP) serves mainly for packaging and common household items like stationary. It is both a plastic and a plastic fiber. Polypropylene is also the safest plastic because of its high melting point.

Polystyrene (PS) can be found as insulation, especially for food packaging. When foamed, it is known as styrofoam, but it generally brittle, transparent, and hard.
Polylactic Acid (PLA) is biodegradable, but it is also toxic is absorbed as a vapor. It is the perfect candidate for handling consumer product and medical implants set to degrade. Unlike other thermoplastics, polylactic acid is derived from fermented plant starch.

Polycarbonate (PC): is used in engineering for it’s clear and easily molded properties. It is also extremely strong, stronger than glass, and lightweight. Police used this for riot shields as an example of its durability.
